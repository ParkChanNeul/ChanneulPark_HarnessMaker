## Adopt

- Korean Connection is a mentor system for understanding Korean people, relationship distance, tone, and cultural meaning.
- Lessons should start from real situations and social pressure, not abstract textbook coverage.
- Student-facing materials should be mission-oriented, action-oriented, visually clear, and usable in live class.
- Role play, real-world mission, next-lesson check, and phone-ready cheat sheet are core operating parts.
- Q/A Drill and Reference flashcard separation is useful for review and fast recall.
- Archive material can preserve rhythm, density, interaction patterns, and visual hierarchy.

## Adapt

- `culture-first` becomes `situation-led` and `culture-explained`; culture explains choices but does not erase grammar progression.
- `grammar as tool` becomes `grammar-tracked`; grammar tools are cumulative learning targets with states and review schedules.
- The single lesson builder responsibility is split into progression planning, lesson architecture, practice design, student experience design, follow-up, assessment, privacy, research, and domain curation.
- Existing module and situation maps become input to progression planning, not direct lesson scripts.
- Existing Quizlet structure is adapted into `quizlet_plan` and `weekly_learning_pack` contracts.

## Reject

- Treating every lesson as a new culture insight exercise.
- Treating one culture explanation as sufficient evidence of learning.
- Letting student deck design remove retrieval, transfer, or independent production.
- Using the old lesson builder as a production dependency for this harness.
- Allowing any agent to change grammar targets outside its contract.
